My Services

What is Individual Therapy

Individual therapy is a type of psychotherapy that involves meeting one-on-one with a trained mental health therapist. This type of therapy can be a helpful way to address a wide range of mental health concerns, including anxiety, depression, relationship issues, trauma, and more. During individual therapy sessions, a therapist will provide a safe and supportive space for an individual to explore their thoughts, feelings, and behaviors. The therapist will work collaboratively with the individual to identify goals and develop a treatment plan that is tailored to their specific needs. Individual therapy may be short-term or long-term, depending on the individual's needs and goals. Short-term therapy typically involves a set number of sessions focused on a specific concern, while long-term therapy can involve ongoing support for individuals who are dealing with more complex mental health concerns. Individual therapy can be a helpful option for anyone who is experiencing mental health concerns, including individuals who are dealing with stress, life transitions, relationship issues, and more. It is important to note that therapy is not a one-size-fits-all approach and may not be suitable for everyone. A trained therapist will assess an individual's specific needs and work with them to develop a treatment plan that meets their goals.

What is CBT?

Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) is a form of psychological treatment that has been demonstrated to be effective for a range of problems including depression, anxiety disorders, alcohol and drug use problems, marital problems, eating disorders, and severe mental illness. Numerous research studies suggest that CBT leads to significant improvement in functioning and quality of life. In many studies, CBT has been demonstrated to be as effective as, or more effective than, other forms of psychological therapy or psychiatric medications.

What is Mindfulness Training?

Mindfulness is rooted in Buddhist and Hindu teachings. Buddhism includes a journey toward enlightenment, and the concept of “sati,”—which encompasses attention, awareness, and being present—is considered the first step toward enlightenment. The term was roughly translated from the ancient language Pali into the term “mindfulness.”

Death and Bereavement Support

If you are struggling with grief and bereavement, please know that you are not alone. Losing a loved one is one of the most difficult experiences we can face in life. The process of grief and bereavement can be overwhelming and isolating, and it is important to have support during this time. Together, we can work towards healing and finding meaning in the midst of loss. Whether you have experienced recent loss or are still struggling with unresolved grief from the past, I am here to provide a safe and compassionate space to process your emotions and work through your grief. During therapy sessions, we will explore the unique challenges of your grief journey, including feelings of sadness, anger, guilt, and anxiety. We will also work together to identify healthy coping mechanisms and self-care strategies to help you manage your emotions and maintain your well-being. My approach to bereavement support is person-centered and tailored to the specific needs and goals of each individual. I understand that everyone's experience of grief is unique, and I will work with you to create a treatment plan that fits your needs.

What is EMDR?

EMDR, or Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing, is a type of psychotherapy that was originally developed to treat post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). Since its inception, EMDR has been used to treat a wide range of mental health concerns, including anxiety, depression, phobias, addiction, and more. EMDR works by utilizing bilateral stimulation, which can include eye movements, hand tapping, or auditory tones, to stimulate both sides of the brain while a person recalls a traumatic or distressing memory. This process is believed to help reprocess the memory and reduce the negative impact it has on the individual. One of the key benefits of EMDR is that it can often produce results more quickly than traditional talk therapy. Additionally, EMDR has been found to be effective in reducing symptoms associated with trauma and other mental health concerns, even in cases where traditional therapies have not been successful.

What is Professional Coaching?

Professional coaching is a type of service that involves working with a trained professional coach to help individuals reach their personal and professional goals. Unlike therapy, which focuses on addressing mental health concerns, coaching is focused on helping individuals achieve success in their careers, relationships, and other areas of their lives. Professional coaching can be helpful for individuals who are looking to make a career change, improve their leadership skills, enhance their communication abilities, or navigate a difficult professional situation. Coaches can provide guidance, support, and perspective to help individuals overcome obstacles and achieve success.
